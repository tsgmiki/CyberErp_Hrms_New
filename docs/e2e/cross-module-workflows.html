Workflow 01

Recruitment → a hired employee

The recruitment pipeline runs a candidate from application to a formal offer, then converts a successful hire into a real employee record — reusing the identity captured at application time.

Recruit & hire Meron Bekele HC077–HC117

  1. Hiring need raised for a Senior Software Engineer in Engineering — submitted & auto-approved (no approval chain configured). Approved
  2. Job requisition created from the approved need, then posted internally & externally. Posted
  3. Candidate registered with consent; her name & gender establish a shared Person record.
  4. Compliance documents uploaded — National ID, Guarantor Form, Medical Certificate, Signed Offer Letter (the hire gate).
  5. Application advanced through Screening Shortlisted Interview, an interview scheduled with a panel, then moved to Selected.
  6. Job offer drafted at 52,000 (with the HC113 salary-deviation justification), submitted → sent → accepted by the candidate. Accepted
  7. Hire — the accepted application is converted to an employee. Gate: offer must be accepted + all compliance docs present
→→
Cross-module link — Recruitment creates the Employee. The employee is built on the candidate's existing Person, so name, contact, education/experience and documents carry over with no re-typing. The candidate is marked hired, the application closes as Hired, and the accepted offer is stamped with the new employee id.
See it in the UI: Recruitment › Hiring Requests → Requisitions → Candidates → Applications → Offers, and the Hire queue. The candidate "Meron Bekele" shows as hired.

Workflow 03

Performance appraisal

Performance evaluates the employee against her position's competencies within a review cycle — and, when finalized, its result feeds Career Development.

Appraise & finalize HC118–HC147

  1. Appraisal generated for Meron in the 2026 Annual Review cycle — seeded with competency lines from her position (Software Design, Communication).
  2. Manager scores each competency 4 / 5.
  3. Finalized — overall score computed as 4.00 / 5 (= 80% normalized). Completed
→→
Cross-module trigger (HC153). Finalizing the appraisal automatically recomputed Meron's succession readiness — no manual action — because a completed appraisal changes her performance history. (Delivered through an extension point, so Performance stays decoupled from Career Development.)
See it in the UI: Performance › Appraisals → Meron's 2026 appraisal, status Completed, overall 4.00.
Workflow 04

Career development & the integrations

Career Development places the employee on a path and a succession plan, and pulls performance + competency data together. This is where the modules visibly connect.

Career path & succession HC148–HC169

  1. Career path "Engineering Leadership Track" (Senior Engineer → Engineering Manager) — Meron assigned, step 1 complete. 50% progress
  2. Succession — the Engineering Manager role flagged critical; a plan created; Meron listed #1 successor, initially Not ready.
↺
HC153 — readiness auto-refreshed. After the appraisal was finalized (Workflow 03), her readiness moved from Not ready to Ready in 1–2 years (56.6%) — competency coverage blended with the fresh 80% performance score.
